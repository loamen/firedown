My pick: A. The single most useful thing a live download card can offer is "stop this," and right now you can't without navigating away. A round ✕ on the trailing edge + a size/percent line turns the card from a passive status readout into something actionable, without the busyness of two buttons. The whole body still taps through to Downloads.

B if you think users genuinely pause downloads often (resume() exists, so it's free functionally — just more UI). C only if speed/ETA is actually computed (I confirmed size+progress, not rate). D if you'd rather not add controls at all and just enrich the readout.

Interaction note for A/B/C: the action button needs its own click target separate from the card-body tap (like the media strip's play/pause vs body split we discussed) — straightforward, but worth deciding the cancel does an immediate abort vs a confirm.
