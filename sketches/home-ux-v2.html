Why this might be the right one

Nothing user-facing moves on Home. The fix lands in the popup, which is exactly where duplication has been quietly accumulating. Browser popup gains structure without losing anything. Risk is essentially zero.

  • Cradle bookmarks stays one-tap on Home
  • Home popup becomes true overflow (History + Settings + Themes + Quit)
  • Browser popup gets scannable: "This page / Library / App"
  • No code that wires bottom-bar slots changes
  • No new affordance for search on bottom (toolbar tap remains the path)
  • Home/Browser bar centres still differ (bookmarks vs FAB) — but that mismatch already ships, and is contextually defensible

Direction 2 — Search cradle, History becomes a card

Search wins the cradle. Bookmarks stays in the popup only on Home (it doesn't earn a card because of low frequency, and the popup is the right home for "library" items). History promotes from popup to a Home card because it's the highest-frequency library destination after Bookmarks. Tapping cradle = focus URL bar + expand autocomplete (same effect as tapping the URL bar — accepted redundancy in exchange for thumb-zone reach).

Why and why not

Search-on-bottom is your thumb-zone win. History gets promoted to a card because of all popup items, it's the one with no other surface on Home and the highest reuse rate. Bookmarks stays popup-only on Home — accepted because the toolbar's URL bar autocomplete already shows recent/top bookmarks inline.

  • Bookmarks doesn't get a useless "42 bookmarks" vanity card
  • History becomes a glance-able destination
  • Home/Browser bar centres still differ visually — search vs FAB — but both are "the unique-to-this-context primary action"
  • Cradle search duplicates the toolbar tap (the unresolved objection)
  • Bookmarks one-tap entry from bottom bar is gone
  • Four cards = a little more scroll on small phones

Direction 3 — URL bar carries more, bar carries less

Push affordances into the URL bar where they already belong. On Browser, the URL bar gets a trailing star button (bookmark this page) — a familiar Chrome/Firefox idiom — removing "Bookmark page" from the popup. On Home, the leading search-glyph stays; we add a trailing bookmark icon that opens the bookmarks list. Cradle on Home stays Bookmarks for thumb-zone access; cradle on Browser stays FAB. Popups shrink.

Why and why not

"Bookmark page" is a context-action on the page itself — it makes more sense in the URL bar where the page is identified, not in a menu where you have to dig for it. Adds Share as a popup item since it's the one common page-action that's currently missing entirely. Home untouched.

  • Bookmarking a page becomes a one-tap UI element on the page
  • Star fills in when bookmarked → glance-able state on every page
  • Home stays exactly as it is — minimal risk on the surface you ship
  • Share fills a real gap (no current share affordance)
  • URL bar gets a second action area; have to make sure long URLs still ellipsize cleanly
  • Doesn't answer the search-button-on-bottom question (we just kept bookmarks cradle)
  • Two new icons to design and wire (★ states + share)

My take, sharper this time

I'd ship D1 + the page-star from D3 together. D1 alone is the safest cleanup — zero surface movement, popup duplication finally gone, Browser popup gets readable sections. The star from D3 then lifts the one page-action that genuinely belongs on the page, not in a menu. Together: small risk, real polish.

I'd hold off on D2 (search cradle / bookmarks demotion) until you have signal. The unresolved objection is real: a bottom search button only adds reach over a URL bar that's already on screen. If you have user evidence that the URL bar gets ignored on Home, D2 becomes worth it; otherwise the slot earns more by staying Bookmarks (which actually gives one-tap to a destination the URL bar can't).

What goes in each popup:

  • Home popup = History · Settings · Themes · Quit (4 items). Nothing reachable elsewhere.
  • Browser popup = sectioned: This page (Find · Desktop site · Share) · Library (Bookmarks · History · Safe Folder) · App (Settings). Bookmark-page hoists to the URL bar star.
